### Boundary Conditions & White Paper Gaps

- Core tension: CAS is a software company, not an enterprise support company — but customers expect end-to-end coverage
- White paper well-received, but consistent feedback: not enough detail on specific boundary conditions
- Key gaps to address:
  - Third-party add-ons (e.g. OpenText) — some basis support is possible, but connectivity and niche areas are not
  - Customer-specific tools (e.g. Control-M for batch/transport management) — if SAP won’t touch it, CAS can’t either; needs clearer pre-qualification
  - Migration/project phases — CAS support begins only once a system exists; deals sold from day one on systems not yet migrated create delivery failure
  - Transport management — functionally owned, but customers expect basis to handle it
- “We don’t support third-party add-ons” is not fully accurate — need nuanced, add-on-specific statements
- Ralph Lauren example: Accenture moved in post-signing, offering more comprehensive coverage → customer paying twice → contract becomes lose-lose

### Messaging & Coaching Gaps

- ASMs and basis leads struggle when presales boundary questions resurface in delivery — not trained for that transition
- ASMs should not be the ones saying “no” to customers — that needs to come from the account team (relationship owners)
- Coaching need: how to frame limitations positively (“what can we do?”) rather than flat refusals
  - Example: CAR and performance monitoring — “it’s excluded” is not a sufficient answer
  - Same issue with Surface Now integration
- One-time training insufficient — needs recurring cadence with live situation-based coaching
- Felipe/Hassan (DHL, Deutsche Post) and Alda (Mercedes) cited as examples of ongoing, account-specific coaching that works
