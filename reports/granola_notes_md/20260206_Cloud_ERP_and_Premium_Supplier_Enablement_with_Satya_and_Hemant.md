### Premium Supplier Enablement Strategy

- C4C acquired by Capgemini, creating new operational structure
  - Control S retains infrastructure ownership (data centers)
  - C4C operations now “C4C a Capgemini company”
  - Back-to-back agreements: SAP ↔ C4C Capgemini ↔ Control S
- Premium supplier enablement required for Cloud ERP delivery
  - All privileged access must go through premium supplier
  - No alternative delivery models allowed
  - Gray area workarounds exist but risk access termination
- Commercial framework unchanged
  - Same order form and conditions across all premium suppliers
  - 70%+ margins maintained by suppliers
  - Need to refresh pricing with Morgenstein’s list prices
- Governance model requirements:
  - TPM role for SLA compliance (supplier side)
  - Supply manager role needed (SAP side) for engagement oversight
  - Similar accountability metrics to CDM model

### Customer Impact Analysis

- Current exposure assessment completed by Hemant:
  - 119 customers on premium supplier (excluding NS2)
  - €4.4M revenue from AO/ASU/CPM deliverable by premium supplier
  - 18 pipeline customers with €9.3M potential (estimated 20-25% conversion)
  - 12 customers with custom scope requiring separate evaluation
- Application Operations Cockpit availability:
  - Only accessible within Cloud ERP packages
  - Not available for standalone AO customers
  - Dashboard provides customer monitoring capabilities
- Risk mitigation built into contracts:
  - Premium suppliers cannot offer SI services in RISE engagements
  - Prevents competition with existing SAP services
  - Revenue flows to ECS if scope expansion occurs

### Reporting Migration & Rollout

- Current SAP Analytics Center status:
  - Most dashboards functional but limited user access
  - Manual upload basis for data
  - Low adoption of new delivery performance dashboards
- BDC migration rationale:
  - Better data accessibility across systems
  - Enables STT and additional data source integration
  - Phase 2 planned for August implementation
- Enablement sessions planned:
  1. Management team session
  2. Report/service owners and delivery managers with cash managers
  3. ASM community session at browser review
- Customer reporting project approach:
  - Dedicated ASM representatives from each region
  - Requirements gathering with Tom’s team
  - Avoid previous reactive approach issues
