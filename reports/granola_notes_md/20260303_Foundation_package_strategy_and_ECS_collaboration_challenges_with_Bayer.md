### Bayer Customer Meeting - Foundation Package Success

- Customer initially resistant to AO Security Office package (€1.2M)
  - Called it “glamorized PMO” - only customer with this specific package
  - Clear feedback: security updates fine, monitoring fine, rest rejected
- Standard foundation pitch delivered - excellent customer reception
  - “This is exactly what we need” - strong positive response
  - Customer willing to eliminate TCS basis team if foundation delivers as promised
  - Currently have larger team than pre-RISE despite being told they could reduce headcount
- Discovery workshop scheduled for post-Easter (4-week lead time)
  - Three-in-a-box approach: ASM, TSM, basis lead
  - PWC consultant to be included
  - Customer committed to providing time and right stakeholders

### Application Operations Cockpit Rollout Gap

- Dashboard live for Cloud ERP customers but poor visibility
  - Bayer customer unaware of existence despite being ideal use case
  - Not included in ASM enablement materials consistently
- Customer reaction extremely positive when shown
  - “This is all what I need” - potential to reduce TCS and Max Attention usage
- Immediate actions needed:
  - Broader rollout to ASM community required
  - Demo sessions needed for sales teams
  - Customer access process for non-foundation customers to be clarified

### ECS-CaaS Collaboration Issues

- Multiple escalation examples (Mercedes, 3M account discovery delay)
  - ASM-basis lead coordination breaking down
  - Ticket-based reactive model vs proactive engagement expectations
  - Customer complaints reaching management level
- Root causes identified:
  - ASMs not joining critical calls or forgetting major accounts
  - Basis leads not proactive enough in customer engagement
  - Role clarity issues between teams
- Remediation approach:
  - Mandatory onboarding call between ASM and basis lead for each customer
  - Joint enablement sessions rather than separate training
  - Regional leadership engagement required

### Foundation Package Documentation Gaps

- Customer-facing materials insufficient for €1M+ deals
  - Missing user profile requirements and authorization details
  - No clear templates for weekly/monthly reporting
  - Advanced job monitoring (Redwood) lacks detailed technical specs
- Current state: draft documents exist but not formalized
- Required deliverables:
  - SAP notes for standard change approvals and user profiles
  - Customer-facing knowledge articles
  - Demonstration templates and visuals
